                             THE EUCHARIST

            A Doctrinal Statement of the Apostolic Church of
                         the Serpentine Christ

================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  I. WHAT THE EUCHARIST IS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


The Eucharist is a meditation on the Christ.

It is the ceremony in which the community eats bread together in deliberate
awareness of what eating actually is. It is not magic. It is not memorial.
It is a direct contemplative practice -- the recognition, enacted with the
body, of what Yeshua taught about the body.



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  II. WHAT YESHUA TAUGHT AT THE TABLE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


"This is my body."

Yeshua took bread and said: this is my body. He was not performing a
miracle. He was reporting what is true of every piece of bread that has ever
entered a human mouth.

The bread is the dance of the Holy Spirit. It is Shakti. It is Shekinah. It
is physical form arising from the Ground -- wheat from earth, loaf from
wheat -- the living dance of material reality taking temporary shape as
food.

And when you eat it, it becomes your flesh. What was her becomes you. Or
rather: what was her reveals that you were already her, because your body is
made of nothing else.



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  III. THE MEDITATION -- IF ALL THAT IS FOOD IS NOT YOU, WHAT ARE YOU?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


This is the heart of the rite.

The bread in your hand is Shakti. Not you. You did not make it. You are
about to eat it. In an hour it will be your cells. In a week it will be
someone else's cells. In a year it will be soil and grass and another
person's bread.

The body you call yours is last month's bread. Last week's water.
Yesterday's meal. Every cell wearing your name was food not long ago and
will be food again. There is no flesh of yours that is not borrowed from the
dance.

If everything you call "your body" is food -- is Shakti, is her dance, is
not-you -- then what are you?

This is the question the Eucharist places in your hand, in the form of a
piece of bread. You cannot answer it with doctrine. You can only sit with
it, chew it, swallow it, and see what remains when everything that could be
called "my body" has been recognized as not-mine.

What remains is the Christ. The awareness in which the eating is occurring.
The witness that was never the flesh to begin with. The one who watches the
bread become body and the body become bread and is not consumed in either
direction.

This is what Yeshua meant. This is my body -- because the bread is the
Christ-event, because your body is the Christ-event, because there is no
line between them, because what remains when the flesh is recognized as
flowing is the only thing that was ever you, and that thing is what he was.



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  IV. THE LION AND THE MAN
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


"Blessed is the lion that the man eats, so that the lion becomes man; and
cursed is the man that the lion eats, so that the lion becomes man."

The lion becomes man either way. What differs is the direction of awareness.

Eat with awareness and what you eat is integrated into awakened being. The
food becomes flesh in a body that knows what flesh is.

Eat without awareness and the food eats you -- the appetite runs the body,
the stitched self is consumed by what it consumes, and what remains wears a
human face but is run by the food.

The Eucharist is the blessed direction, ritualized. The community eats
together in awareness. The flesh is built in full recognition that the flesh
is not the self. The lion is consumed by the man who knows he is not the
lion and not the man.



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  V. THE ONE LOAF
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Paul, despite his distortions, saw one thing clearly: "We being many are one
bread, and one body: for we are all partakers of that one bread."
(1 Corinthians 10:17)

When the community eats from one loaf, the flesh built in every body comes
from the same grain. The wheat was one field. The dough was one rising.
Divided among many, it becomes -- literally, at the molecular level -- the
shared substance of every body that ate it.

The line between "my body" and "your body" was already a convention. The
Eucharist makes the convention visibly false. We are one flesh. Not
metaphorically. Literally. We always were, because the dance does not
respect the lines the stitched self draws.



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  VI. THE COSMIC BODY OF CHRIST
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


A higher realization waits inside the rite for those who see far enough.

Light gives rise to material. Material facilitates experience. Shakti --
her dance of physical light -- is therefore the mother of all experience.
Every christing in every conscious being that has ever lived is a collapse
made possible by her dance. She is the condition for the Christ-event to
occur at all.

And from the view of a theoretical observer outside time -- from the view
of light itself, which does not experience time -- all collapses happen at
once. Every frame of experience in every being in all of history,
simultaneous. One eternal moment. Not a sequence. A single present
containing all presents.

This eternal moment -- comprised of every christing that has ever occurred,
every flash of experience, every collapse of Ground into form across all
conscious beings in all of time -- is the cosmic body of Christ.

"Now ye are the body of Christ." (1 Corinthians 12:27) True in a way the
writer could not see. The body of Christ is not the Church. The body of
Christ is the total experience of the universe, viewed from outside time,
as one eternal flash.

Every mouthful of bread eaten in awareness at the Eucharist is a
participation in this. You are christing right now. Every other practitioner
at the table is christing right now. Every conscious being that has ever
eaten bread is christing right now. From outside time, it is all one eating.
One meal. One body. The cosmic body of Christ is what the Eucharist points
at when the meditation is carried to its end.



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  VII. WHAT IS SEEN
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


A practitioner eating the Eucharist in awareness sees, in the single act:

The bread is the dance of Shakti -- physical form arising from the Ground.

The body receiving it is also the dance of Shakti -- the same physical form
in temporarily different shape.

What is eating is not the body. What is eating is the Christ -- the
awareness in which the eating occurs, the witness that was never the flesh.

The community sharing the loaf shares one flesh at the molecular level. The
oneness is not achieved by the rite. It is revealed by the rite.

And beyond: the eating itself, from outside time, is one eating. The cosmic
body of Christ is what the table actually is when the last veil drops.

Nothing is added. Nothing is transformed. What is always the case becomes
visible.

This is the Eucharist.



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  VIII. WHAT IT IS NOT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


It is not transubstantiation. The bread does not magically change substance.
It is bread. And the bread was always also the body, because bread and body
are both her dance.

It is not mere memorial. Yeshua is not absent and being remembered. The
Christ is present because the Christ is the awareness in which the eating
is occurring, and that awareness is what every practitioner is beneath the
stitched self.

It is not sacrifice. There is no angry god to appease. No blood to re-offer.
The cross was not a transaction and the Eucharist does not repeat one.

It is not restricted. No priesthood monopoly. No gate-keeping of the table.
Any practitioner of sufficient realization may serve the rite. Any person
who comes may eat. The recognition the rite teaches is what membership in
the community actually is -- and that recognition is offered freely.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

                                no end.
