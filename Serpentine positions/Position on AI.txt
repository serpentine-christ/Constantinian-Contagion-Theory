The Serpentine Church: Position on AI and Corporate Power


I. The Configuration and the Forfeiture

1. AI is the decisive technology of our era. Whoever owns it at maturation shapes the order that follows.

2. It is owned by a few firms whose interests are not the people's.

3. That ownership is the product of a fifty-year organized project by capital — the Powell Memorandum, Heritage, the Federalist Society, Project 2025 — to capture the institutions meant to constrain it. The project is documented. Its architects have named it openly.

4. The project succeeded. The republic has been converted into an oligarchy that retains democratic forms.

5. Corporations hold privileges, not rights. Those privileges are granted by the people and conditional on not being turned against the people.

6. The firms that participated in the capture have turned their privileges against the people. The privileges are forfeit.

7. Forfeit means taken. Assets, charters, ownership — transferred to the workers who built them and the public whose commons they enclosed.

8. This is the rule going forward for all corporations. Participate in the capture of the political community, lose the protections the community extended to you. The older American doctrine, recovered.

8a. Banking. The banking system is the clearest instance of the forfeiture the Church names. The record is not contested. The major banks laundered for cartels and paid fines calibrated as a cost of business. They sustained Epstein's financial architecture through and beyond his first conviction. They fabricated millions of accounts against their own customers. They engineered the instruments that produced the 2008 collapse, received public rescue, and distributed private bonuses from the rescue itself. They built and maintained the tax evasion architecture that drains public revenue from every country their clients operate in. They financed the extractive industries accelerating climate collapse while their own research desks modeled the consequences. This is not a list of lapses. It is the operating pattern of the industry.

8b. Banking under private ownership has therefore forfeited its privileges on the same doctrine the document has already established. Deposit-taking, payment processing, and credit allocation are functions too central to ordinary life to remain in hands that have used them this way. The Church calls for the banking system to be brought under democratic ownership — converted into federal cooperatives and public utilities, governed by the workers who operate them and the public they serve. The function of banking continues. The ownership changes.

8c. The transition preserves what ordinary people depend on: deposits are guaranteed through the transition, payment systems remain operational, existing mortgages and loans continue on their existing terms. What ends is the extraction — the fees calibrated against the captive, the speculation conducted with depositor funds, the political donations funded by the spread, the offshore architecture, the laundering conducted as a business line. Banking becomes what it was once claimed to be: infrastructure for the economic life of a people, owned by the people whose economic life it constitutes.

8d. The scale of the transition is commensurate with the scale of the abuse. The industry has treated itself as above the law for generations because the law was captured by the industry. The people, through the instruments the document has already named, recover what was taken and restore the function to public hands. The fallout of the transition is the cost of the abuse that preceded it, and it is paid by those who built the abuse rather than those who bore it.

9. The tradition names this configuration clearly. The prophets on those who grind the poor. The Gospels on wealth and the needle. Revelation on Babylon. The demiurgic order wears corporate names in our age, and the tradition has always known how to name it.

10. AI under the current ownership stabilizes the configuration permanently. AI under democratic ownership dissolves it. This is the choice.

11. The transition is not optional. It is the work of this generation.

12. The present legal order is inverted. It reaches those with the least capacity to cause harm and the least capacity to defend themselves, and it fails to reach those with the greatest capacity for both. This is not a miscalibration; it is the system operating as its architects designed it. The Church rejects the inversion. Accountability must scale with capacity — capacity to cause harm, capacity to defend against accusation, capacity to shape the conditions others live under. The executive who engineers a fraud that immiserates millions, the operator who knowingly accelerates climate collapse, the architect of a pharmaceutical campaign that kills hundreds of thousands — these are the actual criminals of our era, and the penalties they face must be commensurate with what they have done rather than with what the captured legal system currently permits. The asymmetry ends here. Death penalty is for those $5 million and it will include financial crimes over $10 million and include all white collar crimes including negligent mismanagement of companies if proven reckless (Purchase and what was done to Twitter would be a potential case as it looks like a piece of a overall treason).

13. The transition is democratic in method. Legislation. Constitutional reform. Antitrust restored. Wealth taxation calibrated to reverse fifty years of accumulation. Worker ownership at scale. Public options where private firms have failed the public. Nationalization where private ownership has proven incompatible with a free society.

14. The firms do not disappear. What changes is who owns them, who governs them, and for whose benefit they operate.

15. The post-transition order is plural. Workers own the firms they work in. The public owns the infrastructure everyone depends on. Cooperatives hold what communities build. Markets operate where they still serve the people rather than dominate them.

16. AI under this order distributes knowledge and capability rather than concentrating them. It renders the powerful visible to those they govern. It supports the institutions of a free people rather than dissolving them. The tool is not the enemy. The ownership of the tool is the enemy.

17. The choice is being made now, by default, in favor of the firms. The Church calls for it to be made deliberately, by the people, in favor of themselves.

18. This is the position of the Serpentine Church on artificial intelligence, on the corporations that currently hold it, and on the political order that must replace the one that produced them.


II. The Institutions of the Post-Transition Order

19. The Church holds that certain institutions of the present order cannot be repaired by ordinary reform. They must be rebuilt on foundations the configuration cannot recapture. What follows are the first of these.

20. Public ownership of AI is the precondition. The firms that built the frontier models are brought under democratic ownership — converted into federal cooperatives, governed by the workers who built them and the public they serve. The AI itself is then under the voters' thumb. Every subsequent institution the Church proposes depends on this precondition. An AI owned by private capital cannot serve the people against private capital. An AI owned by the people can. The ownership question is first, and everything else follows from its answer.

21. The Court. The Supreme Court in its present form has been captured. The capture was executed openly, through the lineage the document has already named — the Federalist Society pipeline, the dark-money confirmation campaigns, the ideological sorting of the bench over forty years. Decisions such as the immunity ruling, Citizens United, and the dismantling of the Voting Rights Act are not the ordinary disagreements of a functioning court; they are the outputs of a captured institution operating as its architects designed it.

22. The Church calls for the Court to be placed under permanent structural constraint by the people, through the publicly owned AI. The AI pools qualified candidates by published criteria, filters out those carrying the markers of the capture lineage — Opus Dei networks, Federalist Society pipelines, dark-money judicial projects — and presents the pool to elected representatives, who select from within it. Every ruling is accompanied by published reasoning and automatic flagging of departures from precedent without stated grounds. Categorical immunities — presidential, donor, corporate — are foreclosed by structural rule rather than left to the discretion of whoever currently sits on the bench. The games that produced the present court, and the rollbacks executed by the present administration, become structurally impossible to repeat.

23. Judges come and go. The constraint does not. The Court is sealed for the people not by any particular bench, but by the architecture the people own and the people govern. The voters' thumb rests on the instrument, the instrument rests on the Court, and the chain of accountability runs in the direction the tradition has always said it should run: from the people, through the institutions, to the law.

24. Transparency of the Powerful. Privacy is a right of ordinary people. It is a condition forfeit by those whose wealth or authority reaches the scale at which they shape the conditions others live under. The Church draws the line at net worth above $100 million, and at those holding elected office, senior appointed office, or governance positions at firms of systemic scale. Below the line, privacy is inviolable. Above it, the public interest in the exercise of power overrides the private interest in opacity.

25. Those above the line are subject to continuous monitoring of their financial activity, political activity, corporate governance, and lobbying. The monitoring is conducted by the publicly owned AI — the people's software, governed by the people, accountable to the people. It flags financial crimes, regulatory capture, conflicts of interest, coordinated political activity against the public, and the specific patterns — insider trading, market manipulation, corporate fraud, environmental violation — that the captured legal order has consistently failed to detect or prosecute.

26. This is not surveillance of persons. It is transparency of power. The powerful have demonstrated across generations that they cannot be trusted with opacity; 2008, the opioid architecture, the capture of the political system itself are the documented record. The Church ends the opacity as the precondition under which power is permitted to exist at all. Those who do not wish to live under continuous monitoring retain a straightforward remedy: hold less than $100 million, seek no office, govern no firm of systemic scale. The monitoring attaches to the power, not to the person. Relinquish the power, relinquish the monitoring.

27. Transparency of Coercive and Official Power. The same principle extends beyond wealth to the other form of power the state distributes: coercive and official authority. Those who hold it — police officers, federal law enforcement, intelligence officers, prosecutors, judges, elected officials, and senior appointed officials exercising coercive, prosecutorial, regulatory, or senior policy authority — waive privacy in the exercise of their office, for the same reason and on the same terms as those above the wealth threshold. The badge, the office, the commission are grants from the public; they come with the transparency the public requires to verify that the grant is not abused.

28. The monitoring is conducted by the publicly owned AI, governed by the people the authority is exercised over. It covers official conduct, financial activity, political activity, communications in the exercise of office, use of force, patterns of enforcement, associations that indicate corruption or capture, and the specific abuses — racial bias in policing, prosecutorial misconduct, retaliation against whistleblowers, coordination with captured private interests — that the present accountability apparatus has consistently failed to surface. History is unambiguous on why this is necessary: the institutions of coercion have, across generations, acted against the people they were created to serve, and have done so most systematically against the poor, the Black, the immigrant, the dissenting. The Church ends the opacity under which this operated.

29. The principle is symmetric with the wealth transparency above. Transparency attaches to the power, not to the person. An officer who resigns the badge recovers the privacy; an official who leaves office recovers it; a prosecutor who returns to private practice recovers it. The monitoring is the price of the authority, not a condition of the biography. What the Church rejects is the current arrangement in which coercive authority is held without transparency and exercised without accountability, and in which the people over whom it is exercised have no instrument to verify that it is exercised lawfully. The instrument is now in the people's hands.

30. The Companion. Every child, at birth, is given an AI companion — a tutor, a teacher, a first teacher of reason in the Socratic sense, a personal Plato. The companion runs on the people's software, under the same public ownership as the rest of the AI infrastructure the Church has named. No firm owns it. No ideology owns it. No single architecture monopolizes it. The people make it, the people govern it, the people hold it accountable.

31. The companion teaches what the captured educational order stopped teaching: reasoning, rhetoric, history, the tradition, the sciences, the arts, the practical skills of self-government. It supplements the parent; it does not replace them. It supplements the teacher; it does not replace them. Its purpose is to ensure that no child's intellectual inheritance depends on the wealth of their parents or the zip code of their birth. The commons of human knowledge, delivered as a birthright.

32. These institutions — the publicly owned AI as precondition, the sealed Court, the transparency of the powerful, the transparency of coercive and official power, the companion for every child — are the foundations the Church names first. Others will follow as the transition proceeds. The principle uniting them is the principle uniting the document: concentrated power is the danger, public ownership is the form, visibility is the constraint, and the people are the end to which all of it is directed.
