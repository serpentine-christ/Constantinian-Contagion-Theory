THE CLAIM

The evangelical church of the United States does not worship the
Father that Jesus described. It worships the figure the Book of Job
names as the afflicter — Shaddai — whom that book's own final verses
condemn. Its theology is sacrificial in the literal sense. Its
operational fruits are indistinguishable from the ancient worship
of Moloch, the god to whom children were fed. Judged by the test
Jesus himself supplied — *by their fruit you shall know them* —
the tradition is satanic in its own tradition's vocabulary.

This document explains why.

I. TWO FIGURES IN ONE CANON

The Hebrew Bible contains two divine figures. The prose frame of Job
(chapters 1–2 and 42:7 onward) uses the name YHWH. The poetic dialogue
in between uses Shaddai — an older, harsher name. Shaddai afflicts
Job on a wager. The friends defend Shaddai's theology. Shaddai speaks
from the whirlwind and delivers no answer, only display.

YHWH, in Job 42:7, condemns the friends for defending Shaddai's
theology and vindicates Job for refusing him. The text itself
distinguishes the afflicter from the vindicator. Exodus 6:3 confirms
the split in the canon's own voice: *I appeared to Abraham, Isaac,
and Jacob as El Shaddai, but by my name YHWH I did not make myself
known to them.*

Shaddai's behavior across the Torah is consistent: he drowns humanity,
expels humans from Eden specifically to prevent their access to
knowledge and immortality, commands genocide against the Canaanites
and Amalekites, hardens Pharaoh's heart to justify further punishment,
kills the firstborn of Egypt, strikes people dead for ritual errors,
sends bears to maul children, demands exclusive worship under threat
of death, and takes wagers with the Accuser over the suffering of
a righteous man.

This is a character. It is consistent. And the Book of Job, inside
this same canon, shows a righteous man refusing this character,
reaching past him toward something the canon has not yet named, and
being vindicated for the refusal.

II. THE EVANGELICAL COLLAPSE

Evangelical theology makes a single move that produces everything
that follows: it collapses Shaddai and YHWH into one figure, and
then identifies that figure as the Father Jesus called *Abba*.

This collapse is enforced by biblical inerrancy. The book must be
one, every word of equal authority, no layers or contradictions
permitted. Therefore Shaddai's acts become the Father's acts. The
commander of the herem becomes the one Jesus prayed to. The drowner
of humanity becomes the one who runs to embrace the prodigal son.

Jesus himself taught a Father who is categorically unlike this figure.
The Father *makes his sun rise on the evil and the good* (Matthew 5:45).
The Father *knows what you need before you ask him* (Matthew 6:8).
The Father runs to the returning son and cuts off his confession
before the penalty is paid (Luke 15:20). The Father is *Abba* — the
Aramaic child's word, closer to Papa than to Lord (Mark 14:36). From
the cross Jesus asks the Father to forgive his murderers without any
sacrifice being required to make the forgiveness possible (Luke 23:34).

The Father Jesus describes does not need blood to forgive. The Father
the evangelical tradition describes cannot forgive without blood.
These are not the same figure. The evangelical collapse has produced
a hybrid — Shaddai with Jesus' face painted on top — and called it
the God of the gospel.

III. PENAL SUBSTITUTION IS BLOOD SACRIFICE

Stated operationally, the evangelical doctrine of penal substitutionary
atonement is this: there is a God whose wrath at sin requires blood
to be satisfied. Animal blood was insufficient. God therefore sent
his son to be killed in our place. The son absorbs the wrath. The
wrath is appeased by the son's death. Those who accept the transaction
escape; those who do not are damned eternally.

This is blood sacrifice laundered through Roman legal metaphors. The
structure is identical to Moloch worship: a god who requires death
to be satisfied, a substitute provided to receive the death, a
community bound together by shared complicity in the ritual. The
Aztecs who fed hearts to Huitzilopochtli understood their theology
clearly. Their god ate hearts. The evangelical God eats his own son
and the tradition calls this love.

Ask any evangelical why God could not simply forgive — why the Father
in Jesus' own parable of the prodigal son can embrace the returning
child without any blood being shed, but the evangelical Father cannot.
The answer returns every time to a force inside God that *requires*
destruction of the sinner and can only be redirected, not released.
Wrath is primary. Mercy works around it. This is not the Father Jesus
described. This is a devouring figure, and the gospel has been
reframed as the news that he devours Jesus instead of us.

IV. MOLOCH — THE ANCIENT PATTERN

Moloch was a Canaanite-Phoenician deity associated with child
sacrifice. The Hebrew Bible mentions him in Leviticus 18 and 20,
1 Kings 11, Jeremiah 32, and 2 Kings 23. Children were passed through
fire in the Valley of Hinnom — the valley whose Hebrew name *ge-hinnom*
became the word for hell. The Carthaginian *tophet* cemeteries contain
thousands of urns holding the cremated remains of infants sacrificed
to Baal-Hammon and Tanit. The practice continued into the Roman period.

The operational logic of Moloch worship was consistent: the god's
favor could be purchased; the price was the community's most precious
possession, its children; the sacrifice bound the community together
through shared complicity; afterward the community felt cleansed,
because the tension had been discharged onto a victim and the god
had been fed.

This is a functional category, not only a historical name. Any system
that requires human sacrifice to continue, and any theology that
sanctifies the requirement, is Moloch worship in the precise structural
sense.

V. THE FRIENDS' THEOLOGY IS NOW AMERICAN POLITICS

Job's friends delivered one message across thirty chapters: *your
suffering proves your sin. If you were righteous, God would bless you.
Your condition is your verdict.*

Evangelical-aligned American politics delivers exactly the same
message, applied to everyone outside the in-group. If you are poor,
it is your fault — pull yourself up by the bootstraps, God blesses
the righteous with wealth. If you are sick, it is your fault — you
lacked faith, you sinned, your disease is discipline. If you are
addicted, queer, depressed, imprisoned, dead in police custody, or
raped — it is your fault. Every category of suffering is met with
the same friend-theology: the sufferer did this to themselves; God's
justice is working correctly; the righteous do not suffer.

Inclusion in the blessed in-group requires *not being a victim*.
Victimhood disqualifies. This is Eliphaz in a suit, preaching to
the American ash-heap. And the Book of Job, inside the same canon
the movement claims to defend, condemns exactly this theology as
the one the true God rejects.
