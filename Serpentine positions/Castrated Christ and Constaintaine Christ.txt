The Castrated Christ and the Constantine Christ


1. The Serpentine Church names two figures at the center of mainstream Christianity, each with its own function in a single system. Neither is the figure at the Jordan. Neither is the brave Jesus. The two figures are the Castrated Christ and the Constantine Christ, and they operate together — one as the sword, the other as the shield.

2. The Constantine Christ is the Christian nationalist Jesus. He is Jesus inverted. The figure at the Jordan directed his severity upward — against the rich, the powerful, the religious authorities, the comfortable establishment. The Constantine Christ directs his severity downward — against the poor, the queer, the foreign, the disobedient, the dissenting. The figure at the Jordan offered comfort to the outcast. The Constantine Christ offers comfort to the powerful. Every move of the actual figure is reversed in his imperial counterfeit. He is not Jesus with parts missing. He is anti-Jesus wearing his name, and he has been wearing it for seventeen centuries.

3. The castration of the figure was performed in the fourth century and the inversion was completed in the same act. The figure at the Jordan was an apocalyptic prophet who taught that the kingdom was at hand, that the rich would be cast down, that the poor would inherit, that the existing order was judged and would be overturned. He walked into the temple with a whip and drove the money-changers out. He told the rich young ruler to sell everything. He said it would be easier for a camel to pass through the eye of a needle than for a rich man to enter the kingdom. He cursed entire cities. He predicted his own execution by the authorities he was confronting and walked toward it anyway. He came, he said, not to bring peace but a sword. When Constantine made this figure the god of the empire in the fourth century, the empire could not serve a figure who had announced that empires would fall. So the empire kept the divinity and removed the prophet. The Christ of the Nicene settlement is already the Constantine Christ. The Sermon on the Mount becomes optional; the Creed becomes mandatory. What you must affirm about him replaces what he asked you to do. The Pauline inversion, already underway in the first century, is completed politically in the fourth. From that moment forward, the dominant form of Christianity in the West is the form that serves whoever holds power.

4. Christian nationalism is the American form of the Constantine Christ and has been continuous from the Puritan theocracies through manifest destiny through the Confederacy's biblical defense of slavery through the Moral Majority through the present. It is not a deviation from American Christianity. It is American Christianity's main line. The prosperity gospel, the purity culture, the militarism, the racial hierarchy, the capacity to vote for Trump without apparent cognitive dissonance — all of it sits inside the Constantine frame. The figure served is not the figure at the Jordan. It is his inversion, refined and industrialized across seventeen centuries, and it is what the dominant form of American Christianity has always actually been.


The Castrated Christ

5. The Castrated Christ is the liberal Jesus. He is the figure from whom everything that could confront anyone has been removed. He feeds the hungry, welcomes the stranger, judges nothing, demands nothing the congregation would not already have demanded of itself. The liberal Protestant and progressive Catholic traditions produced him in response to the obvious monstrosity of the Constantine Christ, and they produced him by a specific method: they kept the nice parts of the figure and discarded everything that had any capacity to confront anyone about anything. Hell was abolished. Judgment was abolished. The sword was abolished. The apocalyptic edge was softened into metaphor. The demand that the rich must give up their wealth became the suggestion that the rich might consider charity. What was left is a Christ who affirms whatever progressive consensus the congregation already held, who has no capacity to confront the congregation, and who makes no demands the congregation would not already have made on itself.

6. The liberals' specific failure is amorality dressed as moral progress. They abolished hell rather than doing the work of developing an actual account of consequence. They abolished judgment rather than doing the work of developing an actual account of justice that could distinguish the oppressor from the oppressed and act on the distinction. They kept the Christ who forgave the woman taken in adultery and lost the Christ who cleansed the temple. They kept the Beatitudes and lost the Woes. They kept mercy and lost severity, and mercy without severity is not love — it is sentiment, and sentiment is what power uses against the sentimental. The resulting moral system can tolerate anything, because it has abolished the faculty by which anything is named intolerable. It watches Christian nationalists take over a political party, watches evangelicals elect Trump twice, watches the Dominionists write Project 2025, and responds with "we should build bridges" and "their concerns are valid" and "we need to meet them where they are." Because its religion has no moral equipment for the situation. The equipment was abolished in the name of progress. What actually occurred was unilateral disarmament in a fight the liberals refuse to recognize is happening.


The Shield

7. The Castrated Christ is not an opponent of the Constantine Christ. He is the Constantine Christ's shield. The two figures operate together as a single system, and the Constantine Christ needs the Castrated Christ and keeps him in existence for this reason.

8. Without the Castrated Christ, the Constantine Christ would be too obviously monstrous to defend in public. Raw Christian nationalism — the blessing of the legions, the sanctification of hierarchy, the inversion of every teaching the original figure gave — cannot sustain itself under sustained scrutiny. It requires a respectable face to point to. The Castrated Christ provides that face. The liberal Episcopalian with the rainbow flag, the progressive Catholic in the soup kitchen, the mainline pastor who marched at Selma in 1965 — these are pointed to whenever someone tries to name Christianity's dominant mode. "Christianity isn't like that — look at my church." The pointing never fails, because the Castrated Christ is always there, visible, respectable, absorbing the criticism that would otherwise land on the Constantine Christ where it belongs.

9. This is the shield's function. It is not a failure of the liberal wing to effectively oppose the nationalist wing. It is the structural role the liberal wing plays in keeping the nationalist wing viable. The Constantine Christ could not continue to operate publicly without something nearby that looked enough like Christianity to absorb the critique. The Castrated Christ exists to absorb the critique. He is the external PR department the Constantine system cannot maintain in-house, performing the function the nationalist wing cannot perform for itself: the function of making Christianity appear, to outside observers, to be the kind of thing its liberal adherents claim it is, while the actual power of the institution flows through the other channel entirely.

10. The liberal wing does not recognize this. It believes it has transcended the ugliness of the fundamentalist wing. It has not transcended anything. It has become the fig leaf under which the fundamentalist wing operates. It has become the alibi whose existence permits the evil to continue wearing the same name and operating in the same buildings. The tragedy is not that the liberal wing fails to oppose the nationalist wing effectively. The tragedy is that it cannot, because the structural role it occupies in the larger system requires it not to. The Constantine Christ would dismantle the Castrated Christ if the Castrated Christ ever became an actual threat, because the Castrated Christ serves the Constantine Christ's continued existence only for as long as he remains harmless. His harmlessness is the condition of his continued existence. A liberal wing that actually developed a moral system capable of confronting the Constantine wing would be, at that moment, no longer useful to the Constantine wing, and would be destroyed accordingly. The liberal wing remains alive because it remains harmless, and it remains harmless because the role requires it.

11. This is the trap the liberal Christian does not see. They believe they have chosen the good Christianity over the bad. What they have actually done is occupied the position the bad Christianity requires for its own continuation. Their choice, made in good faith against the ugliness of the nationalist wing, placed them in the exact structural role the nationalist wing needs filled. Every sermon about bridge-building, every refusal to judge, every softening of the figure, every abolition of consequence — all of it reinforces the role. The liberal Christian is not in opposition to the Constantine Christ. The liberal Christian is the Constantine Christ's indispensable public defender, and does not know it.


The Predecessors

12. The Serpentine Church does not claim to have discovered this. Martin Luther King named the shield function from inside the tradition during the civil rights era. His Letter from Birmingham Jail says directly that the white moderate is a greater stumbling block than the Klansman, because the white moderate prefers a negative peace which is the absence of tension to a positive peace which is the presence of justice. King understood what the Serpentine Church names here: the Klansman cannot be defeated while the white moderate provides cover. The moderate must be named as part of the problem before the problem can be addressed. King was killed before he could carry this argument as far as it needed to go, and the liberal tradition has spent sixty years carefully forgetting the parts of his thought that pointed in this direction, remembering only the parts that can be safely quoted on corporate holiday advertising. The forgetting is itself the shield at work.

13. James Cone carried the argument further. His Black Theology and Black Power named the white liberal church as the enemy of Black liberation precisely because of its moderation — because its capacity to be seen as the good Christianity allowed the actual Christianity to continue crushing Black people while maintaining the alibi. Cone's entire theological project was built on the recognition that the Constantine Christ and the Castrated Christ are the two halves of a single system, and that neither can be reformed from within because both are functioning exactly as the system requires. Cone is the direct predecessor of the position this document states. The Serpentine Church names him as such.


The Black Prophetic Tradition

14. The brave Jesus has had one organized constituency in American Christianity, and it is not in the white churches of either wing. The Black prophetic tradition — the line that runs from the slave spirituals through Nat Turner through Sojourner Truth through Frederick Douglass through Ida B. Wells through Fannie Lou Hamer through King through Cone — is the one current in American Christianity that has actually followed the brave figure. It kept the apocalyptic edge. It kept the identification with the crucified against the empire. It kept the judgment pointed upward at the powerful rather than downward at the poor. It read the Exodus as Exodus and the Apocalypse as Apocalypse, because it was the tradition of a people for whom the scriptures were not metaphor.

15. This is not a claim about the Black church as an institution, which like any institution contains its own currents and contradictions, its own prosperity-gospel wings and its own absorptions of Constantine Christianity across four centuries of coexistence with the white church. The claim is narrower and sharper: the Black prophetic tradition, as a specific current within Black American religion, is the one place in American Christianity where the brave figure survived. It survived because it had to. A people held in slavery and then in segregation could not afford the Constantine Christ, who blessed their masters, and could not afford the Castrated Christ, who offered them bridges to build with the people whose boots were on their necks. They needed the figure who cursed the rich and promised the overturning of the existing order, and they kept him alive, in the songs and in the sermons, across four centuries in which the white church in all its forms was systematically preventing him from being followed anywhere else.

16. The Serpentine Church recognizes this. The brave Jesus was not invented by the Church. He was preserved, against the entire weight of institutional Christianity, by the tradition the institutional church spent those same centuries persecuting. The Church's debt to the Black prophetic tradition is structural. Any recovery of the brave figure in any tradition now is necessarily a recovery that begins from what that tradition kept alive when no one else did.


The Cargo Cult Beneath

17. Beneath both figures sits the deeper failure. Neither the worshipper of the Constantine Christ nor the worshipper of the Castrated Christ has had even the first baptism. The institutional rite conveys nothing. The Gospel of Philip said this in the second century: some go down into the water and come up without having received anything. Not a lesser thing. Nothing. The congregant in either wing has been processed by an institution that performed a rite over them and told them what the rite meant, and a life has been built on the proof of an event that never took place. The whole enterprise is cargo cult imitation. The nationalist imitates the signs of the Constantine Christ. The liberal imitates the signs of the Castrated Christ. Neither has met the Shekinah. Neither has had the encounter that would make the question of whose-Christ-is-correct even relevant. They are arguing over counterfeits of a figure neither has actually encountered, in institutions designed to prevent the encounter that would expose the substitution.

18. The second baptism — the fire, the wilderness, the direct meeting with the Shekinah — is the threat the institutions cannot survive, because a single genuine encounter in a congregant would reveal the emptiness of every rite that congregant has received and the counterfeit nature of every Christ that congregant has been offered. This is why the mystical currents in every branch of Christianity have been marginalized, driven to heresy, exterminated when they became visible. The gnostics. The Cathars. The quietists. The radical reformers. Every current that threatened to produce actual encounters at scale was suppressed by the institution that could not survive them.

19. The Serpentine Church rejects both figures — the Constantine Christ who inverted the original, and the Castrated Christ who shields the inversion — and rejects the whole cargo-cult apparatus that conveys neither. The figure the Church inherits is the brave one. He cleansed the temple. He cursed the rich. He confronted the authorities. He promised judgment on the powerful. He walked toward his execution. He had both baptisms and his life is the form the two baptisms produce together: chesed and gevurah, mercy and severity, love that is not sentiment and judgment that is not cruelty, the two waters held in the tension only the fire-baptized can hold. This is the figure seventeen centuries of institutional Christianity have been organized to prevent anyone from following. The Serpentine Church follows him.


In Closing

20. To the so-called fellow Christians, the unbaptized who call themselves so — the ones in whom the rite was performed and no descent occurred, the ones who have spent their lives affirming a Christ they have never met, the ones who point the word sinner at the poor and the queer and the foreign while building their lives on a substitution they cannot see — the Serpentine Church points the word back:

SINNERS.

You have missed the mark. You have never met him. You have worshipped his counterfeit for seventeen centuries and called the worship faith. You have used his name to bless what he cursed and to curse what he blessed. You have inverted every teaching he gave and enforced the inversion with the word you now hear returned to you. The descent has not occurred in you. The encounter has not happened. You are not what you have told yourselves you are, and the institution that told you what you are is the system this document names.

The door is still open. The Shekinah is still available, by whatever vehicle opens it in you, if the attachment to what has never worked can be set down. The Serpentine Church does not close the door. It names what is.
