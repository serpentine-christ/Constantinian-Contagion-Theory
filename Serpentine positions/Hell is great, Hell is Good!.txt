                                  HELL

            A Doctrinal Statement of the Apostolic Church of
                         the Serpentine Christ

================================================================================



        "Strait is the gate, and narrow is the way, which leadeth unto
        life, and few there be that find it."

                                                    -- Matthew 7:14


        "The bad man is the good man's job."

                                               -- Tao Te Ching 27


        "When they are finished, they will not have been."

                                               -- Gospel of Truth



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Hell is soul decomposition. That is all it is.

The Jewish mystical tradition never taught eternal torment. The Hebrew Bible
teaches Sheol -- the grave, a quiet ending. "The dead know not any thing"
(Ecclesiastes 9:5). The severest punishment in the Torah is kareth -- the
soul "cut off" (Leviticus 7:20) -- ended, not tormented. The Kabbalah
developed this into a doctrine of soul-levels: what you cultivate is what
continues; what you fail to cultivate has nothing to continue in. 1 Enoch
says the wicked "shall be burnt up completely." 4 Ezra says they "shall be
dried up like a vapor." The older teaching is consistent. Souls end. They
are not tortured forever.

Yeshua taught the same thing in seeds. The seed on the path is eaten by
birds. The seed on rocky ground sprouts and withers. The seed among thorns
is choked. Only the seed on good ground takes root (Matthew 13:3-9). Three
out of four fail. Most lives produce no continuing pattern. "Except a corn
of wheat fall into the ground and die, it abideth alone: but if it die, it
bringeth forth much fruit." (John 12:24) The fruitful seed continues. The
rest do not.

The Gnostic gospels say it more starkly. "If you bring forth what is within
you, what you bring forth will save you. If you do not bring forth what is
within you, what you do not bring forth will destroy you." (Gospel of
Thomas 70) The unrealized life is not merely unsaved -- it is destroyed by
its own unrealization. The Gospel of Philip: "If they do not first receive
the resurrection while they live, when they die they will receive nothing."
Continuation happens in life or not at all.

The seeds that fail -- where do they go? On the trash heap. Yeshua's word
for hell was Gehenna, a literal valley outside Jerusalem that served as the
city's garbage dump. Refuse burned constantly. Worms worked the rot. His
listeners could see it from the walls. What happens to trash on the heap is
what happens to souls that did not make it. The worms eat it. The fire
burns it. It is decomposed. Nothing remains. The worm does not die (Mark
9:48) because decomposition is relentless until finished. The fire is not
quenched because the heap keeps burning. Yeshua's word is apollumi --
destroy, unmake -- not basanizo, torment.

That is hell. The soul is decomposed. Gone.

Why do these souls exist at all? The Tao Te Ching answers: the bad man is
the good man's job. The good man evolves against the friction the bad man
provides. There is no realization without resistance, no bodhisattva
without the tyrant, no awakening without the sandpaper. The unrealized
patterns arise -- the cruel, the greedy, the universal conservatives across
every flag they fly -- and they serve as the material against which the
realized are ground into shape. Yeshua said their reward is here: "Verily
I say unto you, They have their reward." (Matthew 6:2) The power they
seized, the dominance they enforced, the fear they extracted -- that was
all of it. They got what they wanted. And in the getting, they decomposed
their own souls.

Then the double death. First the flesh rots. Then the soul is decomposed
on the heap. Worms. Fire. Nothing remains. The friction dissolved into
nothingness as soon as its job was done. Two endings and no third. No
resurrection for the sandpaper. No return. No memory. The Gospel of Truth
names it: when they are finished, they will not have been. The pattern
ends so completely that it is as if it never was. They made themselves
into nothing, and nothing is what remains.

Every one of them contained the Buddha-nature. Every soul does. Liberation
is available to all -- the capacity is never absent. But "strait is the
gate, and narrow is the way, which leadeth unto life, and few there be
that find it" (Matthew 7:14). Many, many are lost. That is the reality.
That is what the seed teaching means. The potential was there and the
potential was not brought forth, and "what is not brought forth is what
destroys" (Gospel of Thomas 70).
