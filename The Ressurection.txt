                            THE LIGHT-BODY

            A Doctrinal Statement of the Apostolic Church of
                         the Serpentine Christ

================================================================================



        "And Enoch walked with God: and he was not; for God took him."

                                                     -- Genesis 5:24


        "And was transfigured before them: and his face did shine as
        the sun, and his raiment was white as the light."

                                                    -- Matthew 17:2



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


The body is light that has forgotten itself.

Genesis 3:21 says God clothed Adam in kotnot or. The Hebrew word or has two
spellings -- with ayin it means skin, with aleph it means light. Rabbi
Meir's Torah scroll read aleph, and the rabbis preserved the memory in
Genesis Rabbah 20:12. Before the fall, Adam wore a garment of light. His
body was light. The fall was the forgetting -- the moment the light-garment
was experienced as opaque skin, the moment the body was perceived as solid
matter rather than as the dance it always was. The Kabbalah carried this
teaching forward. The Zohar and the Lurianic tradition name the chaluka
de-rabbanan, the garment of light woven by the realizations of a life, worn
after the gross body dissolves. What is cultivated is what continues. This
is identical in substance to what Dzogchen teaches about the rainbow body:
the physical form was always light, realization recovers the seeing of it,
and a fully realized practitioner's body returns to light at death because
nothing else was ever there to return to.

The Hebrew tradition records those who completed the recovery. Enoch did
not die -- "he was not, for God took him" (Genesis 5:24). The Merkavah text
3 Enoch describes what happened: "my flesh was transformed into flame, my
sinews into blazing fire, my bones into coals of burning juniper, the
substance of my body into blazing fire." Elijah ascended alive in a chariot
of fire (2 Kings 2:11) -- the Merkavah mystics read the chariot as the
vehicle of the completed light-body, the form a realized practitioner takes
when the gross body is no longer needed. Moses came down Sinai with "the
skin of his face shone" (Exodus 34:29), karan or panav, sending forth rays
of light that the people could not bear to look at. He veiled himself. The
radiance did not fade quickly. The tradition preserved all of this as
public evidence that the light-garment is real and can be recovered.

Yeshua was a first-century Jewish mystic working inside this tradition, and
he knew it. The Transfiguration (Matthew 17:2) is the same event as Moses
on Sinai -- "his face did shine as the sun, and his raiment was white as
the light" -- Yeshua's light-garment becoming perceptible through the
skin-garment in front of the three disciples who could bear the sight. His
whole ministry was the demonstration of a body in the process of returning
to its light-nature while still alive.

The resurrection completes what Enoch and Elijah completed. The crucifixion
was the willed surrender of the gross body -- "Father, into thy hands I
commend my spirit" (Luke 23:46) -- the final release of the guf back to the
dance. The empty tomb is not a puzzle. The empty tomb is Enoch's empty
death-scene and Elijah's empty ascension-spot. "He is not here: for he is
risen" (Matthew 28:6). There was no corpse because the body had returned
to the light it was always made of. What remained was the chaluka -- the
light-garment Yeshua had woven during his life, as complete a garment as
has ever been woven.

The post-resurrection appearances confirm it. He eats fish (Luke 24:42-43).
He is touched (John 20:27). And he passes through locked doors (John
20:19), is recognized only when he chooses to be (Luke 24:16, 31), appears
and vanishes at will. Mary Magdalene takes him for the gardener until he
speaks her name (John 20:15-16). This is exactly what the Kabbalah says
the chaluka does and exactly what the Dzogchen tradition documents in its
rainbow-body accounts -- the light-body takes whatever form serves the
moment because it is not bound by the patterning of gross matter. The
Gospel of Philip states the doctrine plainly: "Those who say they will die
first and then rise are in error. If they do not first receive the
resurrection while they live, when they die they will receive nothing."
Resurrection is the recovery of the light-body, and it is accomplished in
life or not at all.

Yeshua's resurrection is not a unique metaphysical exception proving a
creed. It is the most complete and most public instance of the light-body
transformation the Jewish mystical tradition had always pointed at and the
Dzogchen lineage has independently confirmed. Enoch completed the
transformation privately. Elijah completed it with witnesses. Moses began
it on Sinai. Yeshua completed it in full view of a movement that would
carry the teaching forward -- the public demonstration that the body is
light, that the garment can be recovered, that what we call death is not
what the skin-eye reports. The resurrection is literal. It really happened.
The body returned to what it always was.
