                              THE TRINITY

            A Doctrinal Statement of the Apostolic Church of
                         the Serpentine Christ

================================================================================



        "I am the light that is above them all. I am the All. The All
        came forth from me and the All attained to me. Split a piece of
        wood, and I am there. Lift up the stone, and you will find me
        there."

                                         -- Gospel of Thomas, Logion 77



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  I. WHAT THE TRINITY IS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


The Trinity is not three gods. It is not a creedal paradox to be accepted
against reason. The Trinity is the accurate description of reality. Three
aspects of one process.

Only three things exist: the Ground of Being, experience, and physical light.



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  II. THE FATHER -- THE GROUND OF BEING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


The Father is the Ground of Being. The term is from Dzogchen, and it is
exact.

It is what Hinduism calls Brahman. What Taoism calls the Tao. What the
Kabbalah calls Ein Sof. What the Sufis call al-Haqq. It is the quantum field
of physics. It is the undifferentiated substrate out of which all definite
reality arises. In Hindu terms, Shiva -- still, unmoving, the ground.

It is not a being. It is not a person. It is the field. It is what is there
before anything definite has arisen, and what remains when everything
definite has dissolved. It has no beginning and no end because beginnings
and endings happen within it.

Scripture witnesses this ground. "The Father which is in secret" (Matthew
6:6) -- in secret because it cannot be perceived as an object, being that
which perceives. "In him we live, and move, and have our being" (Acts
17:28). "I AM THAT I AM" (Exodus 3:14) -- pure being, without predicate,
the ground naming itself. The Gospel of Truth calls it "the incomprehensible,
inconceivable one who is superior to every thought." The Apocryphon of John:
"not something among things which exist, but something greater than these."
The Tripartite Tractate: "a single one... the first one and the one who is
only himself."

This is the Father.



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  III. THE CHRIST -- THE COLLAPSE INTO FORM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


The Christ is the event of the wave becoming particle. The collapse from
undifferentiated potential into definite reality. When the quantum field
resolves into a specific experience, that collapse is the Christ.

This is what incarnation is. Reality being born. Experience arising from the
Ground. The indefinite becoming definite.

Under Penrose's theory, this happens in the brain roughly forty to fifty
times per second. Each collapse is a frame of experience. Each frame is a
christing. You are being christed continuously. Every piece of wood is a
christing. Every stone is a christing. Every breath is a christing. Wherever
the Ground takes definite form, the Christ is occurring.

John opens with exactly this: "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word
was with God, and the Word was God. All things were made by him; and without
him was not any thing made that was made." (John 1:1, 3) "And the Word was
made flesh." (John 1:14) The Word is the ongoing making of flesh itself.

John uses light in the awareness-sense to name this principle: "That was the
true Light, which lighteth every man that cometh into the world." (John 1:9)
Not physical light -- the light of awareness, the consciousness that
illumines every arising. Yeshua extends it: "I am the light of the world."
(John 8:12) "Ye are the light of the world." (Matthew 5:14) The awareness
that illumines is the Christ-principle awake in each of them.

The Gnostic gospels see it plainly.

    "I am the light that is above them all. I am the All. The All came forth
    from me and the All attained to me. Split a piece of wood, and I am
    there. Lift up the stone, and you will find me there."
                                         -- Thomas, Logion 77

    "Whoever drinks from my mouth will become as I am, and I will become
    that person, and the hidden things will be revealed to him."
                                         -- Thomas, Logion 108

    "The kingdom is inside of you, and it is outside of you."
                                         -- Thomas, Logion 3

Inside and outside because the Christ-principle arises everywhere the Ground
takes form.

    "The kingdom of the Father is spread out upon the earth, and men do not
    see it."
                                         -- Thomas, Logion 113

It is already the case. Men do not see it because they are looking for a
future event, not a continuous arising.

Yeshua of Nazareth is the Christ because he knew himself as this principle
and taught from that recognition. He is the Christ, the experience itself.
He was not unique in being christed. He was unique in knowing.

From an observer outside the universe -- outside of time -- all things
happen at once. Every christing across all history occurs in a single
eternal moment. Every collapse from Ground to form, every flash of
experience in every conscious being that has ever existed or ever will, all
of it present simultaneously, beyond sequence. From that view, the material
falls away entirely. What remains is the total experience, all at once,
beyond time.

This is the body of Christ.

The Church fumbled toward this for centuries without seeing it. "Now ye are
the body of Christ." (1 Corinthians 12:27) "There is one body... one God and
Father of all, who is above all, and through all, and in you all."
(Ephesians 4:4-6) True in a way the writers did not fully grasp. The body
of Christ is not a metaphor for the institutional Church. The body of Christ
is the totality of experience, seen from outside time, as the one arising of
the Ground into all its flashes at once.

The Gospel of Truth saw it: "He is the form of the Father, he who existed
before anything and after everything came into being." The Tripartite
Tractate: "The Son... is the form of the formless, the body of the bodiless,
the face of the invisible."

"Before Abraham was, I am." (John 8:58) A report from outside time. The
Christ-principle is what am is.

"That they may be one, even as we are one: I in them, and thou in me, that
they may be made perfect in one." (John 17:22-23) Perfect in one -- all
experience, all christings, unified in the single eternal arising. This was
his prayer. This is what the view from outside time shows.



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  IV. THE HOLY SPIRIT -- THE DANCE OF PHYSICAL FORM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


The Holy Spirit is physical light. Literally. The electromagnetic reality
physics describes. The substance of which all material form is made when
examined closely enough.

In Hindu terms, Shakti -- the active principle, the dance of manifestation.
In Kabbalistic terms, Shekinah -- the indwelling presence, the divine as the
material world itself. She is not a wisdom inside material reality. She is
material reality. The wisdom is inseparable from the dance, because the
dance is inherently intelligent.

She is the unconscious mind -- definitionally. Every intelligence in the
body that operates without the stitched self's involvement is her. The right
hemisphere. The dreaming mind. The healing that closes a wound. The balance
that holds you upright. The intuition that knows before the thought forms.
The immune system recognizing what belongs and what does not. Everything the
organism does that the ego neither authored nor understands is her.

Our interaction with her is constant and unceasing. Every breath, every
heartbeat, every moment of digestion and repair, every dream. You do not
have to approach her. She is already what is running you. The work is only
to notice.

She is in you and out there because these are not two places. The right
hemisphere's silent knowing is her as local. The forest holding itself
together is her as biosphere. The cell knowing how to divide is her as
microscopic. One dance. Physical. Intelligent. Her.

Her dance gives rise to the appearance of material form and to the
experience that arises when the Ground collapses into frame. The material
and the experience are not additional things sitting on top of her. They are
what her dance is, seen from inside. It is just that: dance.

Scripture witnesses her. Ruach -- the feminine breath-wind-spirit -- moves
over the deep at the beginning (Genesis 1:2). "Let there be light" (Genesis
1:3) is her first act: the physical light that is the substance of her
dance. Wisdom in Proverbs is she: "The Lord possessed me in the beginning of
his way, before his works of old." (Proverbs 8:22) The Wisdom of Solomon:
"a pure emanation of the glory of the Almighty... a reflection of eternal
light, a spotless mirror of the working of God." (Wisdom 7:25-26) Thunder,
Perfect Mind speaks in her voice: "I am the substance and the one who has no
substance." Trimorphic Protennoia: "I move in every creature. I am the
Invisible One within the All."

She is Gaia in her local face. She is feminine because the dance of physical
form has always been named Mother -- what holds the world together is not
striving but knowing. She is the Mother. The Earth. The body. The dance.



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  V. WHAT IS REAL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Only three things exist:

    THE GROUND OF BEING. The Father. Shiva. The quantum field. Still,
    undifferentiated, aware.

    EXPERIENCE. The Christ. The ongoing collapse of Ground into definite
    frame. Reality being born, now and now and now. From outside time, all
    frames at once -- the body of Christ.

    PHYSICAL LIGHT. The Holy Spirit. Shakti. Shekinah. The dance of material
    form, which is what matter actually is when correctly seen. The
    unconscious intelligence of the body and the world. The wisdom is the
    dance.

Material does not exist as the solid thing the unawakened eye reports. There
is no additional stuff. There is the Ground, there is the collapse into
experience, and there is her dance of physical light -- and the stitched
appearance of a solid, separate world is an artifact of the stitching.

This is what Yeshua saw. This is what he taught. This is the Trinity.
